An Indigenous public art walk celebrating the enduring presence of Coast Salish peoples on this land.

Welcome to Coquitlam, a city situated on the unceded traditional territory of the kʷikʷəƛ̓əm (Kwikwetlem) First Nation.
The name "Coquitlam" is derived from the kʷikʷəƛ̓əm word meaning "red fish up the river," referring to the sockeye salmon that once flourished in the local waters and served as a vital resource for the Kwikwetlem people. This map guides you through two significant public artworks that visually narrate the profound connection between the Indigenous peoples, the local ecosystems, and the community. By exploring these sites, viewers from all cultural backgrounds are invited to learn about Coast Salish history and the enduring presence of Indigenous stewardship on these lands.
